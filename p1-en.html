A-SO Foot Pressure Innovation Service

This project was conducted in two phases. The first phase used design thinking to identify the needs and pain points of younger consumers regarding dynamic foot pressure measurement services, and proposed feasible solutions. The second phase focused on refining the booking process and interface on A-SO's website, and produced a low-fidelity prototype and usability test plan.

Phase One

Discover

Conducted in-depth user interviews, domain insights, and competitor research to build empathy.

→

Define

Created personas and customer journey maps to define key user needs and pain points.

Develop

Used brainstorming and customer journey mapping to generate potential solutions.

→

Deliver

Gathered user feedback and iterated proposals to complete the final presentation deck.

Phase Two

User Insight Review

Revisited phase one research findings to focus on pain points and goals for refinement.

→

Booking Flow Optimization

Refined the journey map and proposed improvements for the online booking interface.

Interface Design

Created wireframes and developed a low-fidelity prototype iteratively.

→

Usability Testing

Completed a usability test plan including script, SUS survey, and post-test interviews.

Design Outcome

Reflection

This project was my first hands-on experience with design thinking and UX methods. It sparked my interest in the field and helped me realize that good design comes not from creating a perfect solution all at once, but from cultivating empathy and iterating toward a complete, user-centered experience.
