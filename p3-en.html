Master’s Thesis｜The Sustainable Business Model of Social Entrepreneurship

Case Study of Merry Young Social Enterprise

Research Background & Motivation

Non-profit organizations often struggle with funding limitations and scalability. This research explores how NPOs can develop sustainable operations and create social impact by transitioning into social enterprises.

Research Objectives & Questions

  • What innovative service strategies can be applied under resource constraints?
  • How does the role of a social enterprise reshape organizational and market interactions?
  • How can NPOs balance social responsibility, profitability, and long-term sustainability?

Methodology

This qualitative study used semi-structured interviews with the Merry Young team, combined with literature review, business model canvas, and value proposition analysis.

Key Findings & Discussion

  • Introducing market orientation and cross-sector collaboration increased both brand visibility and profitability.
  • Business and social goals were jointly achieved through integrated strategy.
  • Continuous iteration and innovation helped optimize operations and reinforce mission alignment.

Conclusion & Recommendations

  • Social entrepreneurship enables NPOs to achieve both financial viability and social impact.
  • Strengthening branding, stakeholder engagement, and long-term planning is essential for sustainability.
  • Public and government support is crucial to build an enabling ecosystem for social enterprises.
